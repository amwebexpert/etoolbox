# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a **respectful, inclusive, and collaborative environment** for everyone contributing to this project.

Participation in this project—through code, issues, pull requests, or discussions—means agreeing to engage thoughtfully, constructively, and in good faith.

---

## Expected Behavior

We expect all contributors to:

- Be **respectful and courteous** toward others, regardless of background or experience.
- Communicate **clearly, constructively, and professionally**.
- Assume **good intentions**, even when opinions differ.
- Be patient and helpful, especially when reviewing or responding to contributions.
- Focus feedback on **the code or the proposal**, not on the person.

### In Pull Requests and Code Reviews

- Provide **clear, actionable, and relevant comments**.
- Explain _why_ a change is requested when suggesting modifications.
- Prefer questions and suggestions over commands.
- Review with care: read the full context before commenting.
- Keep discussions focused on improving quality, correctness, and maintainability.
- Acknowledge good work when appropriate.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable:

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Dismissive, sarcastic, or hostile language.
- Public or private intimidation.
- Repeated low-effort or non-constructive comments.
- Aggressive reviewing, nitpicking without context, or blocking progress without justification.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories
- Issues and Pull Requests
- Code reviews and comments
- Project-related discussions and communications

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair action in response to any conduct they deem inappropriate.

Actions may include:

- Requesting changes in communication style
- Comment moderation or removal
- Temporary or permanent restriction from participation

---

## Reporting Concerns

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, please reach out to the maintainers privately using the contact method described in the repository’s README.

All reports will be reviewed respectfully and confidentially when possible.

---

## Final Note

We value **technical excellence and human respect equally**.

A welcoming, focused, and diligent collaboration helps everyone do better work—thank you for being part of that effort.
